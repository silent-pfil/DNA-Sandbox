DNA Sandbox
Chapter Two — Building the tools I wished I had to learn about the world around me

What this is
I couldn't learn from textbooks. Diagrams didn't stick. I always thought — if I could just see it, in three dimensions, I would have understood faster.
Chapter One was Quantum Sandbox — atoms, electron clouds, the periodic table, particle collisions.
Chapter Two is DNA. The molecule that wrote you before you were born.

What it does
A free, browser-based 3D visualization tool for exploring DNA — from the four nucleotide bases all the way to your own personal gene sequences.

Tier I · The four nucleotide bases — Adenine, Thymine, Guanine, Cytosine — each shown as a 3D molecular structure with real bond geometry and electron cloud visualization. The alphabet of all life on Earth.
Tier II · The Double Helix — 24 base pairs rendered in full 3D with accurate geometry. Rise of 0.34 nm per base pair, 36° twist, 2.0 nm diameter. Hydrogen bonds between paired bases visible as probability hazes. Drag to spin, pinch to zoom, look at it from any angle.
Tier III · DNA Replication — watch the helicase enzyme unzip the double helix and new complementary strands assemble in real time. Semi-conservative replication, Watson-Crick 1953. Simplified but scientifically honest.
Tier IV · Your Personal DNA — select your eye color and hair color. See the actual gene segments responsible. OCA2/HERC2 for eye color, MC1R for hair. Real chromosomal locations, real variant sequences. Then see what a single base pair mutation looks like — one letter changed, and what it would mean.

No install. No app. One HTML file. Open it in any browser.

The moment that matters
The difference between brown eyes and blue eyes is a single base pair substitution at position rs12913832 on chromosome 15. One A changed to one G. Out of 6.4 billion base pairs. That change is highlighted on screen, in the sequence that represents your traits, in a different color.
That is not a metaphor. That is the actual letter.

What is approximate

Gene sequences shown are representative educational segments — real gene identifiers, real variant positions, illustrative sequences clearly labeled as educational
Molecular geometry is accurate to crystallographic measurements; electron density shown as probability clouds rather than exact orbital calculations
Replication animation is simplified — real enzymology includes primase, Okazaki fragments, proofreading mechanisms not yet visualized
Trait genetics shown for primary determinants; eye and hair color are polygenic and more complex than any single gene can fully capture


The roadmap

Protein folding visualization — watch a gene get transcribed into mRNA and translated into a protein
Full sequence editor — write your own base pair sequence and watch the helix form
Mutation sandbox — introduce mutations and watch their structural effects in real time
Connection to Quantum Sandbox — zoom from the electron bonds in a base pair all the way out to the full helix
